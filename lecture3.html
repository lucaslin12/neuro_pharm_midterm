Key Concepts

  • AMPA vs. NMDA Receptors: Ion permeability, LTP contribution.
  • mGluRs: Group I, II, III (protein coupling, localization).
  • TCA Cycle: Mediates glutamate synthesis; dysfunction leads to E/I imbalance.
  • EAAT Inhibitors: Increase extracellular glutamate (therapeutic potential vs. excitotoxicity risk).
Feature AMPA Receptors NMDA Receptors
Homo- or Heteromers? Heteromers (GluA1-4 subunits) Heteromers (GluN1, GluN2A-D, GluN3 subunits)
Ion Permeability Permeable to Na+ and K+ (Ca2+ for some subtypes) Permeable to Na+, K+, and Ca2+
Role in LTP Mediates initial depolarization Requires both glutamate binding and depolarization (removal of Mg2+ block)
Transmitter Binding Glutamate Glutamate and co-agonist glycine/serine
Membrane Trafficking Regulated by synaptic activity and AMPA-R auxiliary proteins Dependent on synaptic signaling and scaffolding proteins
